Frequently Asked Questions

What is MongoDB Queryable Encryption?
MongoDB Queryable Encryption is a client-side encryption feature that allows you to encrypt sensitive data before it reaches the database server, while still maintaining the ability to perform queries on the encrypted data. This provides maximum security for healthcare applications while preserving functionality.
How does it ensure HIPAA compliance?
Our implementation includes comprehensive audit logging, role-based access control, data encryption at rest and in transit, and automatic compliance reporting. All PHI access is logged and monitored to meet HIPAA requirements for administrative, physical, and technical safeguards.
What types of queries can I perform on encrypted data?
MongoDB Queryable Encryption supports equality queries and range queries on encrypted fields. This means you can search for exact matches, perform comparisons (greater than, less than), and maintain full search functionality while keeping your data encrypted.
How does performance compare to unencrypted data?
Queryable Encryption is optimized for performance and typically adds minimal overhead to your queries. The encryption/decryption happens client-side, and MongoDB's query engine is designed to efficiently handle encrypted data operations.
Can I integrate this with my existing healthcare system?
Yes! Our solution is designed to integrate with existing healthcare applications. We provide APIs, SDKs, and implementation guides to help you add Queryable Encryption to your current MongoDB-based systems with minimal disruption.
What support do you provide during implementation?
We offer comprehensive support including implementation consulting, code reviews, security audits, training for your development team, and ongoing technical support. Our team has extensive experience with healthcare compliance and MongoDB implementations.
